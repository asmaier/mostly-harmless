2.5 Newtonian incompressible fluid

For an incompressible newtonian fluid the terms proportional to the divergence vanish in the stress tensor σi⁢j′. This leads to the following divergence free stress tensor

σi⁢j*=η⁢(∂⁡vi∂⁡rj+∂⁡vj∂⁡ri). (2.106)

2.5.1 Balance equations

Setting the divergence to zero in equation 2.66 we get for the divergence of the stress tensor for an incompressible fluid

∂∂⁡rj⁢σi⁢j*=η⁢∂2∂⁡rj2⁢vi (2.107)

and for

∂∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢σi⁢j*)=η⁢∂2∂⁡rj2⁢(12⁢vi2)+η⁢∂∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡ri) (2.108)

Inserting these results into (2.99)-(2.101) we get as balance equations for an incompressible newtonian fluid

∂⁡vj∂⁡rj=  ⁢0 (2.109)
∂⁡vi∂⁡t+vj⁢∂⁡vi∂⁡rj= -1ρ⁢∂∂⁡ri⁢p+gi+ν⁢∂2∂⁡rj2⁢vi (2.110)
∂⁡e∂⁡t+vj⁢∂⁡e∂⁡rj= -1ρ⁢∂∂⁡rj⁢(vj⁢p)+vi⁢gi+ν⁢∂2∂⁡rj2⁢(12⁢vi2)+ν⁢∂∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡ri) (2.111)

with the so called kinematic viscosity ν=ηρ 1818Be aware that ν is a spatially independent quantity only for incompressible fluids! In the compressible case ρ=ρ⁢(x,t) and therefore also the kinematic viscosity ν=ν⁢(x,t). So do not use ν when dealing with compressible fluid.

2.5.2 Global dissipation of kinetic energy ℰ

Inserting the incompressible newtonian stress tensor (2.106) in the equation for the dissipation of a general incompressible fluid 2.103 yields1919The same result can be obtained by setting ∂⁡vj∂⁡rj=0 and ∂∂⁡t⁢ρ=0 in the equation for the dissipation of a compressible newtonian fluid (2.83).

ℰ=-1V⁢∫Vη⁢(∂∂⁡rj⁢vi+∂∂⁡ri⁢vj)⁢∂⁡vi∂⁡rj⁢𝑑V=-1V⁢∫Vη2⁢(∂∂⁡rj⁢vi+∂∂⁡ri⁢vj)2⁢𝑑V (2.112)

where we used again relation 34.

We can express this result in terms of vorticity by making use of equation (198) which gives us for

η2⁢(∂∂⁡rj⁢vi+∂∂⁡ri⁢vj)2 =2⁢η⁢Si⁢j⁢Si⁢j=2⁢η⁢(Ri⁢j⁢Ri⁢j+∂⁡vi∂⁡rj⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡ri)
=2⁢η⁢(12⁢ωk⁢ωk+∂∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡ri)-vj⁢∂2∂⁡ri⁢∂⁡rj⁢vi)
=2⁢η⁢(12⁢ωk⁢ωk+∂∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡ri)-vj⁢∂2∂⁡rj⁢∂⁡ri⁢vi)
=η⁢ωk⁢ωk+2⁢η⁢∂∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡ri)

So we can express the dissipation for an incompressible newtonian fluid as

ℰ=-1V⁢∫Vη⁢ωk⁢ωk⁢𝑑V-1V⁢∫V2⁢η⁢∂∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡ri)⁢𝑑V (2.113)

Using Gauss’ theorem to transform the second term we can express this like

ℰ=-1V⁢∫Vη⁢ωk⁢ωk⁢𝑑V-1V⁢∮A2⁢η⁢vi⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡ri⁢𝑑A

So if vi⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡ri=0 on the border of the volume V the second term will vanish and the dissipation in a incompressible, newtonian fluid is due to the first term only 2020Have a look at the Appendix .16 for an simple example of a flow field, where the second term doesn’t vanish! 2121Very often ρ is moved to the other side of the equation (which is possible, because we assume it is spatially constant) and so we get ϵ=1V⁢∂∂⁡t⁢∫Vv2⁢𝑑V=-νV⁢∫Vω2⁢𝑑V

ℰ=-ηV⁢∫Vω2⁢𝑑V (2.114)

From looking at the divergence equation (see below) we can derive another relation, which tells us, when the second term in equation (2.113) will vanish

1V⁢∫V2⁢η⁢∂∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡ri)⁢𝑑V =1V⁢∫V2⁢η⁢∂2∂⁡ri⁢∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢vj)⁢𝑑V
=1V⁢∫V2⁢ηρ⁢(∂2∂⁡ri2⁢p+4⁢π⁢G⁢ρ2)⁢𝑑V=2⁢νV⁢∫V(∂2∂⁡ri2⁢p+ρ⁢∂2∂⁡ri2⁢ϕ)⁢𝑑V
=2⁢νV⁢∮A∂∂⁡ri⁢p⁢𝑑A+2⁢ηV⁢∮A∂∂⁡ri⁢ϕ⁢𝑑V

where we made use of the poisson equation for the gravitational potential and Gauss’ theorem. So only if the pressure gradient and the gravitational force balance at the surface of the fluid we can neglect the second term 2222In case of no gravity the pressure gradient has to be zero at the surface..

We hope that from this discussion the assumptions behind equation (2.114) become clear compared to the rather obscure arguments by Frisch (1995). Nevertheless it is still unknown weather our reasoning and the reasoning of Frisch (1995) is equivalent.

2.5.3 Divergence equation

From equation (2.107) we get for

∂2∂⁡ri⁢∂⁡rj⁢σi⁢j*=η⁢∂∂⁡ri⁢(∂2∂⁡rj2⁢vi)=η⁢∂2∂⁡rj2⁢(∂⁡vi∂⁡ri)=0 (2.115)

Using this result in equation (2.104) we get as equation of state for an incompressible newtonian fluid

∂2∂⁡ri2⁢p=-ρ⁢∂2∂⁡ri⁢∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢vj)-4⁢π⁢G⁢ρ2 (2.116)

or in vector notation

Δ⁢p=-ρ⁢∇⁡[∇⋅(vi⁢vj)]-4⁢π⁢G⁢ρ2 (2.117)

Actually the divergence equation for an incompressible newtonian fluid has a very interesting form. It is show in the Appendix .18.3 that the divergence equation might be related to Bernoulli’s law and even to the Einstein equation of general relativity.

2.5.4 Vorticity equation

Inserting equation (2.107) into 2.105 we get the vorticity equation for an incompressible newtonian fluid

∂∂⁡t⁢ωg-ϵg⁢h⁢i⁢∂∂⁡rh⁢ϵi⁢j⁢m⁢vj⁢ωm=ηρ⁢∂2∂⁡rj2⁢ωg (2.118)

or in vector notation

∂∂⁡t⁢𝝎-∇×(𝒗×𝝎)=ηρ⁢Δ⁢𝝎. (2.119)
