2.4 General incompressible fluid

2.4.1 Balance equations

When we talk about an incompressible fluid we mean that the density of the fluid is constant in time, i.e. ∂∂⁡t⁢ρ=0. In most cases it is also assumed that the fluid is not stratified, that means the density is also spatially constant, i.e. ∂∂⁡rj⁢ρ=0. Therefore one could call an incompressible fluid also a constant density fluid.

With constant density the continuity equation (2.56) becomes

∂⁡ρ∂⁡t+∂∂⁡rj⁢(vj⁢ρ) =0 (2.86)
⇔∂⁡ρ∂⁡t+vj⁢∂⁡ρ∂⁡rj+ρ⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡rj =0 (2.87)
⇒∂⁡vj∂⁡rj =0,with ρ≠0 (2.88)

The momentum equation (2.57) and the energy equation (2.58) become

ρ⁢[∂⁡vi∂⁡t+vj⁢∂⁡vi∂⁡rj+vi⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡rj]= -∂∂⁡rip++ρgi+∂∂⁡rjσi⁢j′ (2.89)
ρ⁢[∂⁡e∂⁡t+vj⁢∂⁡e∂⁡rj+e⁢∂⁡vj∂⁡rj]= -∂∂⁡rj⁢(vj⁢p)+vi⁢ρ⁢gi+∂∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢σi⁢j′) (2.90)

If we make use of the relation (2.88) in the momentum and energy equation we finally get as equations for an incompressible, selfgravitating, newtonian fluid

∂⁡vj∂⁡rj=  ⁢0 (2.91)
∂⁡vi∂⁡t+vj⁢∂⁡vi∂⁡rj= -1ρ⁢∂∂⁡ri⁢p+gi+1ρ⁢∂∂⁡rj⁢σi⁢j* (2.92)
∂⁡e∂⁡t+vj⁢∂⁡e∂⁡rj= -1ρ⁢∂∂⁡rj⁢(vj⁢p)+vi⁢gi+1ρ⁢∂∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢σi⁢j*). (2.93)

where σi⁢j* is a divergence free stress tensor.

2.4.2 Divergence equation

In the incompressible case we can set θ=0 and ∂∂⁡ri⁢ρ=0 in equation (2.61) and so we are left with the following equation for an general incompressible fluid

∂2∂⁡ri⁢∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢vj)=-1ρ⁢∂2∂⁡ri2⁢p+1ρ⁢∂2∂⁡ri⁢∂⁡rj⁢σi⁢j*-4⁢π⁢G⁢ρ (2.94)

Solving for p we get

∂2∂⁡ri2⁢p=-ρ⁢∂2∂⁡ri⁢∂⁡rj⁢(vi⁢vj)+∂2∂⁡ri⁢∂⁡rj⁢σi⁢j*-4⁢π⁢G⁢ρ2 (2.95)

or in vector notation

Δ⁢p=-ρ⁢∇⁡[∇⋅(vi⁢vj)]+∇⁡[∇⋅σi⁢j*]-4⁢π⁢G⁢ρ2 (2.96)

2.4.3 Vorticity equation

For an incompressible fluid ∂∂⁡rh⁢ρ=0 and inserting this into the vorticity equation (2.62) we get

∂∂⁡t⁢ωg -ϵg⁢h⁢i⁢∂∂⁡rh⁢ϵi⁢j⁢m⁢vj⁢ωm=1ρ⁢ϵg⁢h⁢i⁢∂∂⁡rh⁢∂∂⁡rj⁢σi⁢j* (2.97)

or in vector notation

∂∂⁡t⁢𝝎-∇×(𝒗×𝝎)=1ρ⁢[∇×(∇⋅σ*~)] (2.98)
