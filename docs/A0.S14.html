.14 Stress tensor in cartesian coordinates for 1d, 2d and 3d

For a so called newtonian fluid it can be shown, that the stress tensor σi⁢j′ in cartesian coordinates in n dimensions is of the form

σi⁢j′=2⁢η⁢Si⁢j*+ζ⁢δi⁢j⁢∂∂⁡rk⁢vk (209)

with Si⁢j* being the symmetric tracefree part of the tensor ∂∂⁡xj⁢vi

Si⁢j*=Si⁢j-1n⁢δi⁢j⁢∂∂⁡rk⁢vk (210)

and Si⁢j being the so called rate-of-strain tensor

12⁢(∂∂⁡rj⁢vi+∂∂⁡ri⁢vj) (211)

In 1d cartesian coordinates the component of the symmetric tracefree part of the stress tensor Si⁢j* is

Sx⁢x=12⁢(∂∂⁡rx⁢vx+∂∂⁡rx⁢vx)-11⁢∂∂⁡rx⁢vx=0. (212)

In 2d cartesian coordinates the components of Si⁢j* are

Sx⁢x =12⁢(∂∂⁡rx⁢vx+∂∂⁡rx⁢vx)-12⁢(∂∂⁡rx⁢vx+∂∂⁡ry⁢vy) (213)
=12⁢(∂∂⁡rx⁢vx-∂∂⁡ry⁢vy), (214)
Sx⁢y =12⁢(∂∂⁡ry⁢vx+∂∂⁡rx⁢vy), (215)
Sy⁢x =Sx⁢y, (216)
Sy⁢y =12⁢(∂∂⁡ry⁢vy-∂∂⁡rx⁢vx). (217)

In 3d cartesian coordinates the components of Si⁢j* are

Sx⁢x =12⁢(∂∂⁡rx⁢vx+∂∂⁡rx⁢vx)-13⁢(∂∂⁡rx⁢vx+∂∂⁡ry⁢vy+∂∂⁡rz⁢vz) (218)
=13⁢(2⁢∂∂⁡rx⁢vx-∂∂⁡ry⁢vy-∂∂⁡rz⁢vz), (219)
Sx⁢y =12⁢(∂∂⁡ry⁢vx+∂∂⁡rx⁢vy), (220)
Sx⁢z =12⁢(∂∂⁡rz⁢vx+∂∂⁡rx⁢vz), (221)
Sy⁢x =Sx⁢y, (222)
Sy⁢y =13⁢(2⁢∂∂⁡ry⁢vy-∂∂⁡rx⁢vx-∂∂⁡rz⁢vz), (223)
Sy⁢z =12⁢(∂∂⁡rz⁢vy+∂∂⁡ry⁢vz), (224)
Sz⁢x =Sx⁢z, (225)
Sz⁢y =Sy⁢z, (226)
Sz⁢z =13⁢(2⁢∂∂⁡rz⁢vz-∂∂⁡rx⁢vx-∂∂⁡ry⁢vy). (227)
